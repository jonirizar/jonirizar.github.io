**Status:** Working paper. Draft available upon request.

This paper re-examines gender differences in retail investors' trading behavior and portfolio performance using population-wide administrative data from Denmark. It shows that men trade more actively than women, but that the welfare consequences of this trading gap are much smaller in the full investor population than in discount-brokerage samples. The analysis also uses inherited stock portfolios to study trading after a plausibly exogenous portfolio allocation, finding that gender differences in performance largely disappear once initial portfolio selection is held fixed.
